# Goethe-Zertifikat B1 -- Exam Overview & Strategy Guide

## Exam Structure

| Module | Duration | Points | Pass Mark |
|--------|----------|--------|-----------|
| Lesen (Reading) | 65 min | 30 points (scaled to 100) | 60% |
| Horen (Listening) | 40 min | 30 points (scaled to 100) | 60% |
| Schreiben (Writing) | 60 min | 100 points | 60% |
| Sprechen (Speaking) | 15 min + 15 min prep | 100 points | 60% |

**Important: Computer-Based Exam**
At Goethe-Institut Berlin (Alexanderplatz) and many other centers, the B1 exam is taken on computer. This means:
- Lesen & Hören: You click to select answers (richtig/falsch, multiple choice, matching)
- Schreiben: You type your emails and texts using a keyboard (German keyboard layout — learn where ä, ö, ü, ß are!)
- Sprechen: Still done face-to-face with a partner and examiners
- You can navigate between questions and change answers before submitting
- A timer is visible on screen

### Key Rules

- **Written exam total:** 2 hours 45 minutes (Lesen + Horen + Schreiben back-to-back)
- **Oral exam:** approximately 15 minutes with a partner, plus 15 minutes preparation time
- Modules can be taken **individually** or **together** in one sitting
- You must score **at least 60%** in EACH module to pass that module
- Each module is graded independently -- you can pass some and fail others
- If you fail one module, you only need to retake that specific module (not the entire exam)
- Results are valid individually, so you can collect modules over multiple exam dates

## Module 1: Lesen (Reading) -- 65 Minutes, 30 Points

You have 65 minutes total for all 5 parts. There is **no extra time between parts** -- you manage your own time.

### Teil 1: Blog or Email -- Richtig/Falsch (6 points, ~10 min)

- **Format:** One longer text (~250 words), personal in tone (blog post, email, online diary)
- **Task:** 6 statements -- mark each as richtig (true) or falsch (false)
- **What they test:** Can you understand the main content and specific details of a personal text?

### Teil 2: Newspaper Articles -- Multiple Choice (6 points, ~12 min)

- **Format:** 2 shorter newspaper or magazine articles
- **Task:** 3 multiple-choice questions per article (a, b, or c)
- **What they test:** Can you understand the main points and details of informational texts?

### Teil 3: Matching People to Advertisements (7 points, ~15 min)

- **Format:** 7 short person descriptions + 10 advertisements (courses, events, products, services)
- **Task:** Match each person to the best advertisement. One person has NO match (answer = x)
- **What they test:** Can you scan and compare multiple short texts to find relevant information?

### Teil 4: Forum Comments -- Pro or Contra (7 points, ~12 min)

- **Format:** 7 people comment on a discussion topic in an online forum
- **Task:** For each comment, decide if the person is **Ja** (pro/in favor) or **Nein** (contra/against)
- **What they test:** Can you identify opinions and positions in informal written texts?

### Teil 5: Instructions or Rules -- Multiple Choice (4 points, ~8 min)

- **Format:** One formal text (house rules, regulations, terms of use, instructions)
- **Task:** 4 multiple-choice questions (a, b, or c)
- **What they test:** Can you understand precise details, conditions, and exceptions in formal language?

### Lesen Points Summary

| Teil | Format | Points |
|------|--------|--------|
| 1 | Richtig/Falsch | 6 |
| 2 | Multiple Choice | 6 |
| 3 | Zuordnung (Matching) | 7 |
| 4 | Ja/Nein | 7 |
| 5 | Multiple Choice | 4 |
| **Total** | | **30** |

## Module 2: Horen (Listening) -- 40 Minutes, 30 Points

Audio is played through speakers for the entire group. You cannot pause or replay -- the recording controls everything, including pauses for reading and answering.

### Teil 1: Short Everyday Dialogues (10 points, ~10 min)

- **Format:** 5 short dialogues (at a shop, on the phone, between friends, etc.)
- **Task:** 2 questions per dialogue -- 1 richtig/falsch + 1 multiple choice
- **Heard:** TWICE
- **What they test:** Can you understand short everyday conversations?

### Teil 2: Monologue or Radio Report (5 points, ~5 min)

- **Format:** 1 longer monologue (radio report, guided tour, voicemail, presentation)
- **Task:** 5 multiple-choice questions (a, b, or c)
- **Heard:** ONCE only
- **What they test:** Can you understand the main points of a longer spoken text?

### Teil 3: Conversation -- Richtig/Falsch (7 points, ~8 min)

- **Format:** 1 conversation between two people (discussion, planning, interview)
- **Task:** 7 richtig/falsch statements
- **Heard:** ONCE only
- **What they test:** Can you follow a conversation and identify specific information?

### Teil 4: Radio Discussion (8 points, ~10 min)

- **Format:** 1 discussion with a moderator and 2 guests on a topic
- **Task:** 8 multiple-choice questions -- identify who says what or what position they hold
- **Heard:** TWICE
- **What they test:** Can you follow a longer discussion and distinguish between speakers' opinions?

## Module 3: Schreiben (Writing) -- 60 Minutes, 100 Points

You type your answers on a computer. Manage your own time across all 3 tasks.

### Aufgabe 1: Informal Email (~80 words, 40 points, ~20 min)

- **Format:** You receive a short situation + an email/message from a friend
- **Task:** Write a reply addressing 3-4 content points given in the instructions
- **Register:** Informal (du-Form, casual tone)
- **Graded on:** Task completion (all points addressed), coherence, vocabulary, grammar

### Aufgabe 2: Forum Comment / Opinion (~80 words, 40 points, ~25 min)

- **Format:** A discussion topic is presented (from an online forum or newspaper)
- **Task:** Write your opinion, including: your position, a reason, an example from experience
- **Register:** Semi-formal (neutral, clear)
- **Graded on:** Task completion, argumentation, coherence, vocabulary, grammar

### Aufgabe 3: Formal Email (~40 words, 20 points, ~15 min)

- **Format:** A formal situation (cancel an appointment, request information, complain)
- **Task:** Write a short formal email addressing 1-2 specific points
- **Register:** Formal (Sie-Form, polite phrases)
- **Graded on:** Appropriateness of register, task completion, grammar

### Schreiben Points Summary

| Aufgabe | Type | Words | Points | Time |
|---------|------|-------|--------|------|
| 1 | Informal email | ~80 | 40 | ~20 min |
| 2 | Opinion/Forum | ~80 | 40 | ~25 min |
| 3 | Formal email | ~40 | 20 | ~15 min |

## Module 4: Sprechen (Speaking) -- 15 min + 15 min Preparation

The speaking exam is done with a partner (another candidate). Two examiners are present: one leads the exam, the other takes notes. You get 15 minutes of preparation time before entering the room.

### Teil 1: Joint Planning (~3 min, 28 points)

- **Format:** You and your partner must plan something together (a party, a trip, a gift)
- **Task:** Discuss and agree on several points (Who? When? Where? What do you need?)
- **What they test:** Can you make suggestions, react to your partner, and reach agreements?
- **Preparation:** You see the task card during prep time. Plan possible suggestions.

### Teil 2: Presentation (~3-4 min, 40 points)

- **Format:** You give a short presentation on a topic (e.g., "Should children use the internet?")
- **Task:** Cover these points: describe the situation in your home country, state advantages and disadvantages, give your personal opinion
- **What they test:** Can you speak freely and coherently on a topic for several minutes?
- **Preparation:** You choose one of two topic cards and prepare your presentation.

### Teil 3: Discussion (~2 min, 16 points)

- **Format:** After your partner's presentation, you ask questions or give feedback
- **Task:** Ask at least one question, react to what your partner said
- **What they test:** Can you engage in a discussion about a presented topic?

### Pronunciation (16 points, assessed throughout)

- Evaluated across all three parts
- Covers: clarity, natural rhythm, correct stress patterns, understandable intonation
- You do not need to sound native -- you need to be clearly understandable

## General Strategy

### Time Management

**Lesen:**
- Do NOT spend too long on Teil 3 (matching) -- it looks hard but gets easier with practice
- If stuck on a question, mark your best guess and move on
- Double-check your selected answers on screen before time runs out

**Horen:**
- Use the pause before each Teil to read the questions carefully
- Write answers immediately -- do not wait until the end
- For parts heard only ONCE (Teil 2 and 3), read all questions before listening starts

**Schreiben:**
- Stick to the suggested time splits: 20 + 25 + 15 minutes
- Aufgabe 3 is worth fewer points but is quick -- do not skip it
- Write a brief outline before each task (1 minute)

**Sprechen:**
- Use ALL 15 minutes of preparation time
- For Teil 2, write bullet points, not full sentences -- you must speak freely, not read

### What Examiners Look For

1. **Task completion:** Did you address all required points?
2. **Coherence:** Does your text/speech flow logically?
3. **Range:** Do you use varied vocabulary and sentence structures?
4. **Accuracy:** Are your grammar and spelling reasonably correct?
5. **Appropriateness:** Is the register correct (formal vs. informal)?

Examiners do NOT expect perfection. They expect B1-level performance: you communicate clearly with some errors that do not block understanding.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Lesen Teil 1:** Confusing "the text mentions X" with "the statement says X" -- read both carefully
- **Lesen Teil 3:** Matching based on only ONE criterion when the person has multiple requirements
- **Horen Teil 2/3:** Panicking when you miss something -- keep listening, the next answer is coming
- **Schreiben:** Writing too little (under 60 words) or too much (over 120 words for Aufgabe 1/2)
- **Schreiben Aufgabe 3:** Using "du" instead of "Sie" in a formal email
- **Sprechen Teil 1:** Giving a monologue instead of interacting with your partner
- **Sprechen Teil 2:** Reading from your notes instead of speaking freely
- **General:** Not answering every question -- there is no penalty for wrong answers, so always guess

### Study Plan Recommendation (4-6 Weeks)

**Weeks 1-2: Foundation**
- Take a full practice exam to identify weak areas
- Study vocabulary lists for each module
- Practice Lesen Teil 3 and Teil 4 daily (these are trainable skills)
- Listen to German podcasts/radio for 20 minutes daily

**Weeks 3-4: Focused Practice**
- Do timed practice for each module separately
- Write 3 practice emails per week (1 informal, 1 opinion, 1 formal)
- Practice Sprechen Teil 2 presentations with a timer (3-4 minutes)
- Review all grammar weak points (Konjunktiv II, Nebensatze, Prapositionen)

**Weeks 5-6: Exam Simulation**
- Take 2-3 full practice exams under real conditions
- Time yourself strictly
- Practice the speaking exam with a partner or tutor
- Review your most common errors and create a personal "mistake list"

**Daily habits throughout:**
- 10 minutes vocabulary review
- 15-20 minutes listening (Deutsche Welle, podcasts, YouTube)
- Read one short German text (news article, blog post)
