> **Note:** German uses a **comma** for decimals (not a period): 3,50 EUR (not 3.50). A period is used for thousands: 1.000 = one thousand.

> **Kulturtipp: Einkaufen in Deutschland**
>
> **Backerei (Bakery):** Germans are very proud of their bread -- there are over 3,000 types of bread in Germany! A traditional bakery (*Backerei*) is found on almost every street corner. You typically order at the counter: *"Ich mochte drei Brotchen, bitte."*
>
> **Supermarkt:** Common chains include ALDI, LIDL, REWE, EDEKA, and Kaufland. ALDI and LIDL are discount supermarkets (*Discounter*). You usually need a coin (50 Cent or 1 Euro) to unlock a shopping cart (*Einkaufswagen*).
>
> **Pfand (deposit):** Most plastic and glass bottles have a deposit (*Pfand*) of 0,08 to 0,25 Euro. You return them at machines (*Pfandautomat*) in the supermarket to get your deposit back.
>
> **Bags:** Bring your own bags! Plastic bags cost money and many shops no longer offer them. Most Germans bring a reusable bag (*Stoffbeutel*).

## 3. Grammatik -- Grammar (10 Minuten)

### 3a. "mochten" im Geschaft -- mochten in Shopping Contexts

You already know *mochten* from Lektion 4. Here it is in shopping situations:

**Useful shopping phrases with mochten:**

| Deutsch | English |
|---|---|
| Ich mochte... | I would like... |
| Ich mochte ein Kilo Tomaten, bitte. | I'd like a kilo of tomatoes, please. |
| Ich mochte drei Brotchen. | I'd like three rolls. |
| Was mochten Sie? | What would you like? (from seller) |
| Mochten Sie noch etwas? | Would you like anything else? |
| Das ist alles, danke. | That's all, thanks. |
| Sonst noch etwas? | Anything else? |
| Nein, danke. Das ist alles. | No, thanks. That's all. |

> **Note:** After quantities (ein Kilo, eine Flasche, etc.), the noun does NOT need an article:
> - *ein Kilo **Kartoffeln*** (not ~~ein Kilo die Kartoffeln~~)
> - *eine Flasche **Wasser*** (not ~~eine Flasche das Wasser~~)

### 3b. Pluralbildung -- Plural Forms of Nouns

German nouns form their plurals in different ways. There are **5 main patterns**. Unfortunately, you mostly need to memorize which nouns follow which pattern, but there are some helpful tendencies.

| Pattern | Ending | Example (Singular -> Plural) | Tendency |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1: -e | add -e (often with Umlaut) | der Tisch -> die Tisch**e** | many masculine nouns |
| 2: -er | add -er (often with Umlaut) | das Kind -> die Kind**er** | many neuter nouns |
| 3: -en / -n | add -en or -n | die Tomate -> die Tomate**n** | most feminine nouns |
| 4: -s | add -s | das Auto -> die Auto**s** | foreign words |
| 5: -- | no change (sometimes Umlaut) | der Kuchen -> die Kuchen | masc./neuter with -el, -en, -er |

**Important: ALL plural nouns use the article "die" (regardless of their singular gender).**

**Detailed examples from our food vocabulary:**

| Pattern | Singular | Plural | English |
|---|---|---|---|
| -e | der Saft | die S**a**fte | juices |
| -e | der Fisch | die Fische | fish (pl.) |
| -e | der Salat | die Salate | salads |
| -er | das Ei | die Eier | eggs |
| -er | das Glas | die Gl**a**ser | glasses |
| -en/-n | die Kartoffel | die Kartoffel**n** | potatoes |
| -en/-n | die Tomate | die Tomate**n** | tomatoes |
| -en/-n | die Banane | die Banane**n** | bananas |
| -en/-n | die Flasche | die Flasche**n** | bottles |
| -en/-n | die Dose | die Dose**n** | cans |
| -en/-n | die Suppe | die Suppe**n** | soups |
| -s | die Cola | die Cola**s** | colas |
| -s | die Pizza | die Pizza**s** (or Pizzen) | pizzas |
| -- (no change) | der Apfel | die **A**pfel | apples |
| -- (no change) | der Kuchen | die Kuchen | cakes |
| -- (no change) | das Brotchen | die Brotchen | rolls |

> **Tip:** When you learn a new noun, always learn THREE things: **article + noun + plural**.
> Example: **der Apfel, die Apfel** (the apple, the apples)

### 3c. Preise -- Asking About and Stating Prices

**Asking the price:**

| Deutsch | English | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Was kostet...? | What does ... cost? | singular items |
| Wie viel kostet...? | How much does ... cost? | singular items |
| Was kosten...? | What do ... cost? | plural items |
| Wie viel kosten...? | How much do ... cost? | plural items |

**Examples:**
- *Was kostet **das Brot**?* -- What does the bread cost?
- *Wie viel kostet **ein Kilo Tomaten**?* -- How much does a kilo of tomatoes cost?
- *Was kosten **die Apfel**?* -- What do the apples cost?
- *Wie viel kosten **drei Brotchen**?* -- How much do three rolls cost?

**Answering about prices:**

| Deutsch | English |
|---|---|
| Das kostet 2,50 Euro. | That costs 2.50 euros. |
| Die kosten 3,99 Euro. | They cost 3.99 euros. |
| Das macht 7,80 Euro. | That comes to 7.80 euros. |
| Das macht zusammen 12,30 Euro. | That's 12.30 euros altogether. |

> **Kulturtipp: Zahlen (Paying)**
>
> Germany is still a cash-heavy society compared to many other countries, though card payments are becoming more common. Many smaller bakeries, markets, and even some restaurants are *"nur Bargeld"* (cash only). It's always good to carry some cash (*Bargeld*).
>
> When paying, the cashier may say: *"Das macht 5,60 Euro"* (That's 5.60 euros). You hand over money, and they might ask *"Haben Sie es passend?"* (Do you have exact change?).

## 4. Ubungen -- Exercises (15 Minuten)

### Ubung 1: In der Backerei -- Dialogue

Read the dialogue, then practice it aloud.

> **Verkauferin:** Guten Morgen! Was mochten Sie?
>
> **Kunde:** Guten Morgen! Ich mochte vier Brotchen und ein Brot, bitte.
>
> **Verkauferin:** Weissbrot oder Vollkornbrot?
>
> **Kunde:** Vollkornbrot, bitte. Was kostet das Vollkornbrot?
>
> **Verkauferin:** Das Vollkornbrot kostet 3,20 Euro. Sonst noch etwas?
>
> **Kunde:** Ja, ich mochte auch ein Stuck Apfelkuchen.
>
> **Verkauferin:** Sehr gern. Sonst noch etwas?
>
> **Kunde:** Nein, danke. Das ist alles.
>
> **Verkauferin:** Also, vier Brotchen, ein Vollkornbrot und ein Stuck Apfelkuchen. Das macht zusammen 7,90 Euro.
>
> **Kunde:** Hier, bitte. (gives 10 Euro)
>
> **Verkauferin:** Und 2,10 Euro zuruck. Vielen Dank! Schonen Tag noch!
>
> **Kunde:** Danke, gleichfalls! Tschuss!

**Vocabulary from the dialogue:**

| Deutsch | English |
|---|---|
| die Verkauferin | saleswoman |
| der Kunde | customer (male) |
| Weissbrot | white bread |
| Vollkornbrot | whole grain bread |
| Sonst noch etwas? | Anything else? |
| Das ist alles. | That's all. |
| zuruck | back (as change) |
| Schonen Tag noch! | Have a nice day! |
| Gleichfalls! | Likewise! / You too! |
