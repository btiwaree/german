# Lektion 1: Hallo und Willkommen!

**Kurs:** Deutsch A1.1 | **Woche 1, Lektion 1** | **Dauer:** 45 Minuten  
**Thema:** Sich vorstellen, Begrussungen, erste Verben  
**Lernziele:** After this lesson you will be able to greet people, introduce yourself, ask and answer basic personal questions, and use the formal/informal distinction in German.

---

## Teil 1: Einstieg (5 Minuten)

### Your Very First German Words

Welcome to German! Let's start with something you probably already know:

| German | English | Pronunciation |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| Hallo! | Hello! | HAH-loh |
| Ja | Yes | yah |
| Nein | No | nine |
| Danke | Thank you | DAHN-kuh |
| Bitte | Please / You're welcome | BIT-tuh |
| Entschuldigung | Excuse me / Sorry | ent-SHOOL-dee-goong |

Try saying each word out loud three times. German pronunciation is more consistent than English -- once you learn the rules, you can read almost any word!

> **Kulturtipp:** Germans shake hands when meeting someone for the first time, even in casual settings. A firm handshake with direct eye contact is standard. Close friends may hug or give a quick kiss on the cheek, but this varies by region.

## Teil 2: Wortschatz (10 Minuten)

### 2.1 Begrussungen -- Greetings

| Deutsch | English | When to use |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| Hallo! | Hello! | Anytime, informal |
| Guten Morgen! | Good morning! | Until ~10:00 AM |
| Guten Tag! | Good day! | ~10:00 AM to ~6:00 PM |
| Guten Abend! | Good evening! | After ~6:00 PM |
| Gute Nacht! | Good night! | When going to bed |
| Tschuss! | Bye! | Informal |
| Auf Wiedersehen! | Goodbye! | Formal |
| Bis bald! | See you soon! | Informal |
| Bis morgen! | See you tomorrow! | Informal |

> **Kulturtipp:** In southern Germany, Austria, and parts of Switzerland you will hear **"Gruss Gott!"** (lit. "Greet God") instead of "Guten Tag." In Switzerland, **"Gruezi!"** is common. These are not religious -- they are simply regional greetings.

### Special Characters (Sonderzeichen)

| Character | Name | Sound | Example |
|-----------|------|-------|---------|
| A a | A-Umlaut | /ɛ/ -- like "eh" | M**a**dchen (girl) |
| O o | O-Umlaut | /ø/ -- like "er" in British English | sch**o**n (beautiful) |

## Teil 4: Grammatik (10 Minuten)

### 4.1 Personalpronomen -- Personal Pronouns

| Person | Deutsch | English | Notes |
|--------|---------|---------|-------|
| 1st singular | **ich** | I | always lowercase (except at start of sentence) |
| 2nd singular (informal) | **du** | you | for friends, family, children |
| 3rd singular masculine | **er** | he | |
| 3rd singular feminine | **sie** | she | |
| 3rd singular neuter | **es** | it | |
| 1st plural | **wir** | we | |
| 2nd plural (informal) | **ihr** | you (all) | informal plural |
| 3rd plural | **sie** | they | same as "she" -- context tells you! |
| 2nd formal (sing. & pl.) | **Sie** | you (formal) | always capitalized |

> **Kulturtipp:** The formal **Sie** (always capitalized) is used with strangers, older people, in professional settings, and with anyone you are not on first-name terms with. When in doubt, use **Sie**. The other person may say *"Wir konnen uns duzen"* ("We can use 'du' with each other") to switch to informal.

### 4.2 Verb Conjugation -- Present Tense

German verbs change their ending based on the subject. The base form (infinitive) usually ends in **-en**.

**Regular pattern:**

| Pronoun | Ending | Example: wohnen (to live) |
|---------|--------|--------------------------|
| ich | -e | ich wohn**e** |
| du | -st | du wohn**st** |
| er/sie/es | -t | er wohn**t** |
| wir | -en | wir wohn**en** |
| ihr | -t | ihr wohn**t** |
| sie/Sie | -en | sie/Sie wohn**en** |

### Key Verbs for This Lesson

**heissen (to be called / named)**

| Pronoun | Conjugation | Translation |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| ich | heisse | my name is / I am called |
| du | heisst | your name is |
| er/sie/es | heisst | his/her/its name is |
| wir | heissen | our names are |
| ihr | heisst | your (pl.) names are |
| sie/Sie | heissen | their/your (formal) name(s) are |

**kommen (to come / to be from)**

| Pronoun | Conjugation |
|---------|-------------|
| ich | komme |
| du | kommst |
| er/sie/es | kommt |
| wir | kommen |
| ihr | kommt |
| sie/Sie | kommen |

**sprechen (to speak)** -- irregular: e changes to i for du and er/sie/es

| Pronoun | Conjugation |
|---------|-------------|
| ich | spreche |
| du | spr**i**chst |
| er/sie/es | spr**i**cht |
| wir | sprechen |
| ihr | sprecht |
| sie/Sie | sprechen |

**sein (to be)** -- completely irregular, must be memorized

| Pronoun | Conjugation |
|---------|-------------|
| ich | **bin** |
| du | **bist** |
| er/sie/es | **ist** |
| wir | **sind** |
| ihr | **seid** |
| sie/Sie | **sind** |

### 4.3 Word Order

**Statements:** The verb is always in the **second position**.

```
Ich heisse Maria.           (I am called Maria.)
Position: [1:Subject] [2:Verb] [3:Rest]
```

**W-Fragen (information questions):** The verb is also in the **second position**, right after the question word.

```
Wie heissen Sie?            (What is your name?)
Position: [1:W-Word] [2:Verb] [3:Subject/Rest]
```

**Ja/Nein-Fragen (yes/no questions):** The verb comes **first**.

```
Kommen Sie aus Deutschland?  (Do you come from Germany?)
Position: [1:Verb] [2:Subject] [3:Rest]
```

## Teil 5: Dialoge (Ubungen, 15 Minuten)

### Dialog 1: Formal (Sie) -- At a Language School

> **Frau Muller:** Guten Tag! Ich bin Frau Muller. Wie heissen Sie?  
> **Herr Kim:** Guten Tag, Frau Muller. Ich heisse Kim Min-ho. Mein Vorname ist Min-ho.  
> **Frau Muller:** Woher kommen Sie, Herr Kim?  
> **Herr Kim:** Ich komme aus Sudkorea, aus Seoul.  
> **Frau Muller:** Ah, schon! Und was sprechen Sie?  
> **Herr Kim:** Ich spreche Koreanisch und Englisch. Und ein bisschen Deutsch!  
> **Frau Muller:** Sehr gut! Willkommen im Deutschkurs!  
> **Herr Kim:** Danke schon!

### Dialog 2: Informal (du) -- Two Students Meeting

> **Anna:** Hallo! Ich bin Anna. Wie heisst du?  
> **Tom:** Hi! Ich heisse Tom. Woher kommst du?  
> **Anna:** Ich komme aus Osterreich, aus Wien. Und du?  
> **Tom:** Ich komme aus England, aus London.  
> **Anna:** Cool! Was machst du hier?  
> **Tom:** Ich lerne Deutsch. Und du?  
> **Anna:** Ich studiere hier. Tschuss, Tom! Bis morgen!  
> **Tom:** Tschuss, Anna!
